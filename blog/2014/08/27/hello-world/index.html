I set up my first blog / personal webpage today. I registered this domain, set up a github page, puzzled over DNS terminology, and toyed around with various tools (metalsmith, wintersmith, jekyll) before finally settling with Octopress / Jekyll.

Dawwww.

I was hoping to use a node.js tool to avoid that whole Ruby thing which is what drove me to look into metalsmith and wintersmith in the first place. But, in light of my inexperience with building webpages, and my use case (publishing my inane thoughts for nobody to read, with minimal programming), I decided to bite the bullet and go ahead with Jekyll/Octopress. I mean, look at those dang stars (<16k and <8K, respectively). If there’s anything I’ve learned in my notable 26 years (and god knows how I struggle to learn anything at all), it’s that by definition the masses are always right.

And about that whole Ruby thing. I kid. At least one of my professional role models (role model is probably too strong a term, considering this is in the context of my professional life) is/seems-to-be categorizable as (we have never spoken) a Rubyist. iOS development puts food in my mouth and a roof over my head (forensic evidence would indicate that I live and eat like a homeless person), and dim as I am, even I can see how much the iOS dev community has benefitted from that Ruby thing. Even if they do prefer to write in squiggly python.
